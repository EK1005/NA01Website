Energy Saving Tips

Simple, practical ways to reduce your household electricity consumption without getting overwhelmed.

Heating & Cooling

Optimize Your Thermostat

Setting your air conditioner just 1°C warmer in summer (or cooler in winter) can reduce your running costs by up to 10%.

Appliances

Wash with Cold Water

About 80-90% of the energy your washing machine uses goes entirely into heating the water. Switching to cold cycles saves significantly over time.

Lighting

Switch to LED Bulbs

LEDs use roughly 75% less energy and last up to 25 times longer than traditional incandescent lightbulbs.

Standby Power

Turn Off Standby Devices

Appliances left on standby (like gaming consoles, TVs, and microwave clocks) account for roughly 10% of household energy usage.

Kitchen

Check Fridge Seals

Ensure your refrigerator doors seal tightly. A faulty door seal allows cold air to escape, forcing the compressor to work overtime.

Insulation

Seal Up Draft Cracks

Keep windows and external doors tightly sealed using draft stoppers to lock down your climate control without wasting power.
